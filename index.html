Elyse Schoenig, News Reporter

Journalism allows me to invest in others and reveal their curiosities, fears, and interests. As a reporter, I help people become the voices of their own narratives. I am dedicated and organized, communicative and positive, and guided by the truth in all I do.

From the dinner table, to the stage, to the field, I thrive on communicating with others and bringing people together. I’m a family and friend oriented person, which pushes me to consider all perspectives in every situation. I grew up as a competitive gymnast and singer, which makes delivery and performance feel like home. I was born and raised in the Midwest, and these roots show in my work ethic. I crave learning more about life through experiences with other people.

Journalism allows me to pursue my love for telling other people’s stories. I believe journalism is necessary to educate, debate, and connect. My genuine interest in people and their experiences is a lifelong path I’m eager to follow.

I’m currently a senior at the University of Missouri-Columbia. I’m set to graduate in May, 2022, from the School of Journalism with an emphasis in Television Reporting/Anchoring. I’ve been a reporter and producer/anchor for KOMU 8 News, Columbia’s NBC affiliate for four years.

I’ve also been a certified drone pilot for nearly a year. Flying drones allows me to use another point of view to tell stories, inform the public, and enhance my watchdog role as a journalist. Incorporating drone flights into my journalism allows me to bring a unique experience to daily stories, feature stories, breaking news and more.

When I’m not working, you can find me on the water with my family, spending quality time with my friends, singing, playing piano, exercising, reading, journaling, going on walks, or watching gymnastics.

-Elyse Schoenig
